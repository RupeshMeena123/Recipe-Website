Dal Tadka

Dal Tadka is a popular Indian dish made with lentils and spices. It is a staple in Indian households and is often served with rice or roti. This recipe will help you make a delicious Dal Tadka at home.

Ingredients:

  • Yellow lentils (toor dal)
  • Onions
  • Tomatoes
  • Ginger
  • Garlic
  • Spices
  • Green chilies
  • Coriander leaves
  • Desi Ghee

Instructions

1. Rinse and pressure cook the toor dal with water, turmeric, and a little salt until soft.
2. In a pan, heat ghee or oil and add mustard seeds and cumin seeds.
3. Add chopped garlic, ginger, and green chilies. Sauté until fragrant.
4. Add onions and cook until golden brown.
5. Add tomatoes, red chili powder, and cook until tomatoes turn soft.
6. Pour this tempering into the cooked dal and mix well.
7. Simmer for a few minutes. Garnish with fresh coriander.
8. Serve hot with steamed rice or roti.

Tips:

- You can use a mix of dals like moong dal and masoor dal for extra flavor.
- Always use fresh ghee for the best aroma.
- Adjust the spice levels according to your taste.

Poha (Flattened Rice)

Poha is a light, healthy, and easy-to-make Indian breakfast dish made from flattened rice, flavored with spices, peanuts, and fresh coriander. It is very popular in Maharashtra, Madhya Pradesh, and many other parts of India. Perfect for a quick and satisfying meal.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups Poha (flattened rice)
  • 1 onion (finely chopped)
  • 1 green chili (finely chopped)
  • 1/4 cup peanuts
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 8-10 curry leaves
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 tablespoon sugar (optional)
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • Fresh coriander leaves (for garnishing)
  • 1 lemon (for squeezing on top)

Instructions

1. Rinse the poha gently in water and drain completely. Set aside.
2. Heat oil in a pan. Add mustard seeds and let them splutter.
3. Add curry leaves and peanuts. Fry until peanuts turn crunchy.
4. Add chopped onions and green chilies. Sauté until onions turn translucent.
5. Add turmeric powder and mix well.
6. Add the soaked poha, salt, and sugar. Mix everything gently.
7. Cover and steam on low heat for 2-3 minutes.
8. Garnish with chopped coriander leaves and squeeze lemon juice on top before serving.


WatchOut Tutotial

Tips:

- Do not over-wash the poha, otherwise it becomes mushy.
- You can add boiled potatoes or peas for extra taste.
- Always serve hot with a sprinkle of sev or fresh coconut for added crunch.

Gulab Jamun

Gulab Jamun is a classic Indian dessert made from khoya (milk solids) and soaked in sugar syrup. It is a favorite at festivals and celebrations. This recipe will help you make soft and delicious Gulab Jamun at home.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup khoya (mawa)
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1 cup water
  • 1/2 teaspoon cardamom powder
  • Oil for frying
  • Chopped nuts (for garnishing)
  • Rose water (optional)

Instructions

1. In a bowl, mix khoya, all-purpose flour, and baking soda to form a soft dough.
2. Divide the dough into small balls.
3. Heat oil in a pan for frying.
4. Fry the balls on low heat until golden brown.
5. In another pan, boil sugar and water to make a syrup.
6. Add cardamom powder and rose water to the syrup.
7. Soak the fried balls in the warm syrup for at least 2 hours.
8. Garnish with chopped nuts before serving.


WatchOut Tutotial

Tips:

- Make sure the oil is not too hot while frying, or the balls will burn.
- Soak the Gulab Jamun in warm syrup for better absorption.
- You can add saffron strands to the syrup for extra flavor.

Rasgulle

Rasgulle are soft and spongy Indian sweets made from chhena (fresh cheese) and soaked in sugar syrup. They are a popular dessert in Bengali cuisine and are loved for their melt-in-the-mouth texture. This recipe will help you make delicious Rasgulle at home.

Ingredients:

  • 1 liter milk
  • 1/4 cup lemon juice
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 4 cups water
  • 1/2 teaspoon cardamom powder
  • Chopped nuts (for garnishing)
  • Rose water (optional)
  • 1/4 teaspoon saffron strands (optional)

Instructions

1. Boil the milk and add lemon juice to curdle it.
2. Strain the curdled milk through a muslin cloth to separate the chhena.
3. Knead the chhena until smooth and form small balls.
4. In a pan, boil sugar and water to make a syrup.
5. Add cardamom powder and saffron to the syrup.
6. Add the chhena balls to the boiling syrup and cook for 10-15 minutes.
7. Let them cool in the syrup for a few hours.
8. Garnish with chopped nuts and serve chilled.
9. You can add rose water for extra flavor.
10. Serve chilled or at room temperature.
11. Enjoy the soft and spongy Rasgulle with your family and friends.


WatchOut Tutotial

Tips:

- Make sure to knead the chhena well for a smooth texture.
- Use fresh lemon juice for better results.
- Soak the Rasgulle in warm syrup for better absorption.
